Pricing strategy

Three decisions every SaaS leader must get right

01
What to charge for
Choose a value metric that scales with customer success — seats, API calls, data processed, outcomes delivered. Misalignment here creates churn.
02
How to structure tiers
Good/Better/Best packaging guides buyers to the right plan and protects margin. Each tier should have a clear buyer persona and upgrade trigger.
03
How to go to market
Self-serve, sales-assisted, or marketplace-led. Your GTM motion must match your buyers' procurement preferences — especially in enterprise.
05 / 14
Pricing models

Five dominant SaaS pricing approaches

Flat rate
Single price for full access. Simple to buy, simple to sell. Hard to capture value at scale.
e.g. $499/month all-in
Per seat
Price per user. Predictable, easy to budget. Revenue grows with adoption — but can discourage broad rollout.
e.g. $30/user/month
Usage-based
Pay for what you consume. Lowers entry barrier, aligns cost to value. The dominant model for AI and data workloads.
e.g. $0.002/API call
Tiered
Price changes at usage thresholds. Rewards high-volume customers, smooths expansion revenue curve.
e.g. first 10K calls free, then $0.001
Feature-based
Gate premium capabilities behind higher plans. Drives upsell through feature discovery and business need.
e.g. SSO, audit logs on Enterprise

09 / 14
Financial health

Pricing decisions live in your income statement

NRR target
>120%
Net revenue retention above 120% means your existing book grows without a single new logo. Pricing expansion drives this.
LTV:CAC ratio
3:1
Minimum healthy ratio. Usage-based models improve LTV as customers grow into the product — CAC stays fixed.
Payback period
<18mo
Time to recover CAC from gross margin. Tier packaging with a clear upgrade path compresses this meaningfully.
Gross margin
70–80%
Target for scalable SaaS. Infrastructure cost optimization on AWS (reserved capacity, Graviton) protects margin as usage scales.

Getting started

A 90-day roadmap to execution

Days 1–30
Instrument and measure
Define your value metric — what usage correlates to customer success
Instrument with Amazon Kinesis + Lambda metering pipeline
Baseline current COGS per customer to protect margin
Audit current pricing vs. competitor landscape
Days 31–60
Package and test
Design Good/Better/Best tier structure with clear upgrade triggers
A/B test pricing page with real prospects
Map features to tiers — save SSO, audit logs for Enterprise
Set NRR, LTV:CAC, and payback period targets
Days 61–90
Activate and scale
List on AWS Marketplace with usage-based billing
Enable Private Offers for enterprise deals
Co-sell activation with your AWS partner development manager
Launch expansion playbook targeting existing accounts
