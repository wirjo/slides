Pricing strategy

Three decisions every SaaS leader must get right

01
What to charge for
Choose a value metric that scales with customer success — seats, API calls, data processed, outcomes delivered. Misalignment here creates churn.
02
How to structure tiers
Good/Better/Best packaging guides buyers to the right plan and protects margin. Each tier should have a clear buyer persona and upgrade trigger.
03
How to go to market
Self-serve, sales-assisted, or marketplace-led. Your GTM motion must match your buyers' procurement preferences — especially in enterprise.

Pricing models

Five dominant SaaS pricing approaches

Flat rate
Single price for full access. Simple to buy, simple to sell. Hard to capture value at scale.
e.g. $499/month all-in
Per seat
Price per user. Predictable, easy to budget. Revenue grows with adoption — but can discourage broad rollout.
e.g. $30/user/month
Usage-based
Pay for what you consume. Lowers entry barrier, aligns cost to value. The dominant model for AI and data workloads.
e.g. $0.002/API call
Tiered
Price changes at usage thresholds. Rewards high-volume customers, smooths expansion revenue curve.
e.g. first 10K calls free, then $0.001
Feature-based
Gate premium capabilities behind higher plans. Drives upsell through feature discovery and business need.
e.g. SSO, audit logs on Enterprise

Cost attribution — AWS SaaS Factory

Two strategies to measure cost per tenant

Less invasive · fast to implement
Coarse-grained
Approximate tenant cost from activity share — if Tenant A is 10% of active users, allocate 10% of charges
Good for siloed models where resources map directly to one tenant
Suitable for early-stage products and pooled architectures with uniform tenants
Lacks precision — won't reveal noisy neighbours or margin outliers
Cost & Usage Reports
Resource tags
CloudWatch
Detailed instrumentation · full attribution
Fine-grained
Instrument at application and infrastructure layer — capture API counts, DB operations, compute time per tenant
Publish metrics as events; aggregate into per-tenant cost records in near real-time
Enables precision pricing, margin defence, and proactive throttling of outlier tenants
Required for usage-based and outcome-based billing at scale

Agentic AI — pricing implications

Agents don't have seats. Per-seat pricing breaks.

Traditional SaaS
Human uses software
Users have seats. Usage is bounded by working hours. COGS is near-zero per seat. Per-seat pricing captures expansion through headcount.
Gross margin: 80–90%
The 2026 renewal cliff
2025 AI pilots priced for adoption. 2026 production contracts must reflect delivered value. Soft ROI positioning doesn't survive renewal. Source: Bessemer Venture Partners
Agentic AI
Agent acts autonomously
No seats — agents run 24/7, execute multi-step processes independently. Every invocation has real inference COGS: model tokens, tool calls, memory reads, execution time.
Gross margin: 50–60% without active cost management
The implication
Pricing must shift from access to outcomes. The COMPASS framework (AWS + Zuora + Simon-Kucher) provides a structured approach: map scope of work against attributability to find your pricing lever.

AI monetisation — Bessemer Venture Partners + AWS

Three charge metrics for AI products

01
Consumption
Per token · per API call
Mirrors your infrastructure economics. Predictable margin, easy to model. Best for technical buyers who understand token counting.
Challenge: buyers think in outcomes, not tokens. Confusing for non-technical enterprise buyers.
Margin predictability
Value alignment
02
Workflow
Per task · per completed job
Charges for discrete, recognisable work units — book a meeting, analyse a spreadsheet, process a document. Dramatically clarifies the value proposition.
Best of both: buyers understand what they're buying, you can model costs per workflow type.
Margin predictability
Value alignment
03
Outcome
Per resolution · per result
Maximum value alignment. Charges only when the AI delivers a measurable result. Highest willingness-to-pay — and highest cost variability.
Real examples: Intercom $0.99/ticket resolved · EvenUp per legal package · Leena AI per ticket closed
Margin predictability
Value alignment

Hybrid wins in practice: platform fee (2× delivery cost) + outcome credits — provides predictability while capturing expansion upside
